# Antitrust Statement

EVP/1 is an open specification published under [CC-BY-4.0](./LICENSE) for the purpose of enabling interoperable, vendor-neutral brand-name clearance verdicts. This document establishes the antitrust posture the editorial body operates under.

## Posture

The editor and any future working-group participants:

- **MUST NOT** use position on the editorial body or working group to favor Etymolt's commercial interests in any normative decision.
- **MUST NOT** propose normative changes whose primary effect is to disadvantage a competitor's implementation.
- **MUST NOT** discuss pricing, market allocation, or commercial terms in any official EVP/1 forum (GitHub issues, PRs, comment-period digests, working-group meetings).
- **MUST** disclose commercial affiliation when proposing normative changes.
- **MUST** treat conformance criteria identically across all issuers, regardless of commercial relationship to Etymolt.

## What's covered

This statement applies to:

- All discussions in this repository (issues, PRs, discussions).
- All comment-period submissions during 2026-06-10 through 2026-09-10.
- All working-group meetings convened under EVP/1 governance (current and future).
- All editorial decisions about specification content, conformance criteria, and protocol evolution.

## Out of scope

- Etymolt's commercial product pricing, business model, or proprietary data corpora — not topics for EVP/1 forums.
- Third-party EVP/1 issuers' commercial offerings — they govern their own.
- Implementation choices (language, license, architecture) of any specific implementation — those are the implementer's decisions.

## Reporting

If you believe a decision violates this posture, file an issue with the `governance` label, or email `hello@etymolt.com` with the subject line "Antitrust concern". The editor will publish the concern + response in the next weekly comment-period digest.

## Why this matters

EVP/1's value depends on multiple independent issuers adopting the protocol. A specification that quietly serves one issuer is a specification no one else will implement. The asymmetric upside of EVP/1 — becoming the protocol everyone signs against — only materializes if every implementer can trust that the spec is governed neutrally. This document is the binding commitment to that posture.
